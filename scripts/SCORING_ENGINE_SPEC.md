# PharmaGuide Scoring Engine Specification

> Production scoring engine: **4.1.0**
> V4 quality configuration: **1.0.4-sports-subtypes**
> Legacy scaffolding configuration: **3.6.0**
> Export schema: **2.0.0** / **110 core columns**
> Last verified against code: **2026-07-15**

## 1. Scope

This specification defines the scoring behavior that is allowed to ship. Code
remains authoritative when this document and implementation disagree.

Primary implementation:

- `score_supplements_v4.py`
- `scoring_v4/router.py`
- `scoring_v4/gate_safety.py`
- `scoring_v4/gate_completeness.py`
- `scoring_v4/modules/`
- `scoring_v4/quality_score.py`
- `scoring_v4/export_adapter.py`
- `build_final_db.py`

The production score is v4 `/100`. `score_supplements.py` still produces live
legacy scaffolding but does not own the public score, ranking, tier, or display.

## 2. Non-negotiable invariants

1. `quality_score_v4_100` is the only canonical public score.
2. `quality_score_status` governs whether a numeric score may ship.
3. BLOCKED/UNSAFE products never ship a rankable score.
4. NOT_SCORED products never enter the live catalog as ordinary products.
5. `raw_score_v4_100` is audit-only and never fills a suppressed public field.
6. `score_100_equivalent` and its display field mirror v4; they are not a
   conversion of the legacy score.
7. Deprecated `/80` columns remain absent.
8. One v4 router owns module dispatch.
9. One shared scoring-input contract owns which ingredient rows reach scoring.
10. Matching and clinical identity decisions occur upstream; scorers do not
    introduce fuzzy identity fallbacks.
11. Every output includes reproducible engine/config/classification provenance.
12. Per-product regression deltas must be attributable before snapshots move.

## 3. Inputs

V4 consumes the enriched product contract generated by
`enrich_supplements_v3.py`. It does not parse raw DSLD labels independently.

### 3.1 Shared ingredient input

`scoring_input_contract.py` is the only scoring-row boundary. It consumes
cleaner-owned role/eligibility fields and emits:

- score-eligible ingredient rows
- stable identity and source linkage
- blend/parent-total/display-only exclusions
- mapped coverage
- diagnostics and strict contract metadata

The same contract is used by pre-score validation, legacy scaffolding, v4, and
export diagnostics. A downstream consumer may explain an exclusion but may not
reclassify the row with a second heuristic.

### 3.2 Required upstream domains

Depending on category, the scorer reads structured blocks including:

- ingredient quality and canonical identity
- product taxonomy/classification
- dose and serving exposure
- safety signals and regulatory applicability
- RDA/AI/UL assessments
- clinical evidence
- proprietary blend/disclosure evidence
- certifications, COA, testing, GMP, and traceability
- manufacturer violations
- probiotics, omega-3, sports, and formulation-specific metadata

Missing optional evidence lowers confidence or pillar credit. Missing usable
identity/payload can trigger NOT_SCORED. The scorer must never invent evidence
to avoid a null.

## 4. Execution model

`score_product_v4()` executes these layers in order:

1. route product to one module
2. evaluate canonical safety signals
3. short-circuit hard safety conditions
4. evaluate class-aware completeness
5. short-circuit unusable products
6. run exactly one category module
7. apply completeness policy and verdict precedence
8. evaluate confidence
9. attach engine/config provenance
10. assemble six public pillars, score, tier, and status
11. overlay public fields once at the export seam

The input dict is not mutated.

## 5. Routing

`scoring_v4/router.py::class_for_product()` is the sole v4 dispatcher.

| Priority | Route | Primary evidence |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | `probiotic` | Product-level probiotic identity, strains, and guarded CFU/name evidence |
| 2 | `multi_or_prenatal` | Prenatal intent plus true multi/B-complex taxonomy or a broad prenatal panel |
| 3 | `b_complex` | Focused B-vitamin panel without disqualifying competing actives |
| 4 | `multi_or_prenatal` | Multivitamin taxonomy or guarded broad-panel fallback |
| 5 | `sports` | Sports protein/active panel plus product intent or native primary-dose evidence |
| 6 | `fiber_digestive` | Fiber, prebiotic, or digestive-enzyme identity |
| 7 | `omega` | Omega taxonomy or primary EPA/DHA evidence |
| 8 | `generic` | Genuine fallback |

Routing prefers canonical taxonomy and panel composition. Product-name signals
are bounded and guarded. The router does not import the legacy scorer.

## 6. Safety gate

`identity/safety.py` normalizes raw evidence into the stable safety-signal
contract. `gate_safety.py` applies policy to `match_resolution`, status,
jurisdiction, and role; it does not branch on matcher implementation names.

| Evidence | V4 action |
|---|---|
| Confirmed banned | BLOCKED; scoring short-circuits |
| Confirmed recalled | UNSAFE; scoring short-circuits |
| Confirmed/likely high-risk or watchlist | CAUTION; score math continues |
| Likely banned/recalled | CAUTION + review; no automatic hard block |
| Review-only/low-confidence | Audit/review metadata; no unsupported verdict |
| Inactive `excipient_acceptable` warning | Visible warning; no false hard block |
| Disease claim | CAUTION |
| Material indeterminate folate UL | CAUTION + review without asserting exceedance |

Primary verdict applicability is US federal/state. Regional evidence remains in
`jurisdictions` and `regional_advisories`.

The final export also enforces banned-substance parity. If export evidence is
broader than the native v4 gate, `suppress_v4_for_hard_block()` collapses the
public contract to BLOCKED + null score; a banned product can never retain a
finite ranking value.

## 7. Completeness gate

`gate_completeness.py` is class-aware. It separates unusable data from honest
but incomplete disclosure.

Hard completeness failure:

- emits `v4_verdict=NOT_SCORED`
- emits `quality_score_status=not_scored`
- emits no public score or pillars
- is quarantined by the final export gate

Soft missing evidence can include dose, CFU, EPA/DHA detail, form factor, or
coverage debt. It is represented in the breakdown/confidence/pillar result and
does not justify fabricated values. The shared `mapped_coverage` source is used;
the v4 gate does not recalculate a competing definition.

## 8. Category modules

All routes are active and score-producing:

- generic
- probiotic
- multi/prenatal
- B-complex
- sports
- fiber/digestive
- omega-3

Modules return structured dimensions, components, penalties, metadata, and
raw score evidence. Category-specific math belongs inside the module/config,
not in the router or exporter.

## 9. Public pillar assembly

`scoring_v4/quality_score.py` builds:

| Pillar | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Formulation | 20 |
| Dose | 20 |
| Evidence | 20 |
| Transparency | 15 |
| Verification | 15 |
| Safety/Hygiene | 10 |

For a scoreable product:

```text
quality_score_v4_100 = clamp(0, 100, round(sum(pillar.score), 1))
```

Category adapters map module evidence to an achievable, purpose-fit pillar
scale. Public quality caps may proportionally reduce pillar scores when a
module supplies a tested cap with provenance. Caps never raise a score.

Clean-label flags remain orthogonal to regulatory verdicts: they may produce a
small bounded Safety/Hygiene deduction and consumer explanation without
claiming that a legal additive is a banned ingredient.

### 9.1 Tiers

| Score | Tier |
|---:|---|
| 95–100 | Elite |
| 90–94.9 | Excellent |
| 80–89.9 | Strong |
| 70–79.9 | Acceptable |
| 55–69.9 | Weak |
| 0–54.9 | Poor |

Tier is null when the public score is suppressed or not scored.

## 10. Verdict and status

Verdict precedence is deterministic:

```text
BLOCKED > UNSAFE > NOT_SCORED > CAUTION > POOR > SAFE
```

For non-blocked v4 module results, the current POOR threshold is 40.0. A carried
CAUTION outranks POOR/SAFE. Completeness policy may impose a tested cap or
CAUTION ceiling without changing the underlying module breakdown.

| Status | Allowed verdicts | Public number |
|---|---|---|
| `scored` | SAFE, POOR, CAUTION | finite |
| `suppressed_safety` | BLOCKED, UNSAFE | null |
| `not_scored` | NOT_SCORED | null |

## 11. Dose and UL contract

### 11.1 Exposure range

- adequacy uses `per_day_min`
- UL/safety uses `per_day_max`
- all units enter the shared canonicalizer before conversion
- demographic references are retained in `data_by_group`
- `reference_profile` identifies the adult-neutral compatibility summary

### 11.2 Folate lineage

This pipeline applies the folic-acid UL only to an identified folic-acid
contribution.

| Label evidence | Adequacy | Folic-acid UL |
|---|---|---|
| Explicit folic acid contribution | Use verified DFE/exposure rule | Compare identified folic-acid contribution |
| Explicit mcg DFE folinic/folinate/leucovorin | Retain DFE adequacy | Not applicable |
| Bare mcg folinic/folinate/leucovorin | Unknown; `scoring_eligible=false` | Not applicable; do not guess a folinic UL |
| Unknown-form folate | Use only supported adequacy evidence | Indeterminate; material cases emit a review flag |

Absent or inconsistent `%DV` is not a conversion. Any future `%DV` recovery
must be a named rule with consistency checks and dedicated tests.

## 12. Confidence and provenance

Every v4 result carries:

- scoring engine version
- classification schema version
- module route
- scoring mode
- every consumed rubric version/fingerprint
- confidence band and drivers for eligible products
- gate-specific blocked confidence for hard failures

Provenance is emitted even for early safety/completeness returns so a dispute
can reconstruct the exact policy state.

## 13. Legacy boundary

The legacy `score_supplements.py` v3.6.0 process remains live because final DB
construction still consumes diagnostics such as review-queue sections,
historical flags, selected detail fields, and strict scoring metadata.

`export_adapter.overlay_v4_scored()`:

1. scores the enriched product with v4 exactly once
2. copies the legacy scored dict
3. refreshes shared-contract diagnostics from current enriched rows
4. reconciles stale legacy flags to v4 evidence
5. writes v4 verdict, `/100` compatibility mirrors, and reserved `_v4_*` data
6. preserves only the legacy internals still required by downstream audit/detail

This boundary provides one public score authority, but it is not yet one
physical scoring implementation. Removing the legacy engine requires proving
and migrating every live scaffolding consumer; it must not be deleted based on
import counts alone.

## 14. Export contract

`build_final_db.py` owns final schema validation and quarantine.

- Export schema is `2.0.0`.
- `products_core` currently has 110 columns.
- Ranking and dedup use `quality_score_v4_100` only for status `scored`.
- Suppressed safety rows may ship with null score and visible verdict/evidence.
- NOT_SCORED rows are quarantined.
- Detail blobs emit `quality_pillars_v4`, score status, provenance, safety,
  confidence, and category explanations.
- Deprecated `/80` export columns are forbidden.

## 15. Change control

Every scoring, identity, safety, RDA/UL, or config change requires:

1. a RED regression at the owning boundary
2. the smallest owner-level implementation
3. focused GREEN verification through `scripts/test.sh fast`
4. full fast verification at the cluster boundary
5. release/full profiles before publication
6. a fresh candidate snapshot
7. per-product score/status/verdict/module/pillar review
8. explicit expected-change records
9. reviewed scoring snapshot re-freeze

No aggregate score summary can replace per-product review. No clinical data
identifier is accepted without content verification against its primary source.

## 16. Required regression surfaces

At minimum, preserve coverage for:

- router precedence and cross-category misroutes
- safety signal normalization and jurisdiction applicability
- hard suppression/null-score behavior
- completeness and mapped-coverage ownership
- malformed-row containment
- proprietary blend dedupe and display-only linkage
- unit canonicalization and serving ranges
- folate/folinic adequacy and UL lineage
- v4 pillar math and export adapter
- absence of deprecated `/80` fields
- per-product scoring snapshots

Supported test entrypoints:

```bash
scripts/test.sh fast
scripts/test.sh release
scripts/test.sh full
```
